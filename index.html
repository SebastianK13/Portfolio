MORE ABOUT ME

I was born in 1995 in a small village in Podkarpackie voivodeship. I graduated with a bachelors degree in computer science at Catholic University of Lublin. Later I have started work in Service Desk as a junior user contact specialist. Experience which I've earned on my position helped me to get essential knowledge about problems which users may encounter and the ways to eliminate those problems. After 8 months of work in SD I have decided to get fully focused on programming, in that time I came back to studies too.

During last 2 years I've made huge advances in programming, learning a lot about C# and Microsoft technologies. From someone whose attitude was mainly negative to programming I've became a programming maniac. My current main purpose is to still make progression in Microsoft technologies, learning new frameworks and creating new more ambitious projects. Untill now I have created a couple of bigger and smaller non-commercial projects.

While my abilities grew, so has the quality of my code. From my point of view learning how to program is learning how to resolve any problems you might encounter on your path. Since coming to that realisation I have decided to change my learning methods. Instead of watching hours of tutorials I have started to work on more and more complicated projects and seeking information about elements which I got stuck on. In that way I have learned a lot about the programming language itself and its mechanism. No more I was looking for cheap workarounds but spent time and effort necesary to resolve the problem. I think that it was and is the most important thing about learning programming.

In my spare time I like to play board games with my mates. I am a huge fan of Czech Republic and Czech language. I can speak a little of Czech language and in my view it is one of the the most interesting languages in the world. I used to be a historical reconstructionist of early middle-ages years ago, so I would call myself a huge fan of history too. Sometimes I can even cook something and it won't necessarily taste totally terrible.
